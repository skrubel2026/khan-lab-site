Associate Professor · Pharmaceutical Sciences

Dr. Ferdous Khan

Associate Professor, Department of Pharmaceutical Sciences. Research centers on metabolic disease — dyslipidemia, diabetes, and non-alcoholic fatty liver disease (NAFLD) — investigated through plant-derived and antioxidant compounds, animal disease models, and molecular and biochemical analysis. This page exists so that colleagues, collaborators, and university admissions committees can quickly confirm a student's research background with this lab.

"We look at metabolic disease from both ends — screening what a compound does to the body, and using molecular tools to understand why it does it. Every student who passes through this lab leaves having worked across that whole pipeline."

About Dr. Khan

Position

Associate Professor, Department of Pharmaceutical Sciences, North South University

Education

Ph.D., The United Graduate School of Agricultural Sciences, Tottori University, Japan (2016). Doctoral research focused on the molecular mechanisms of adipogenesis, particularly the specific action of prostacyclin at different stages of cultured adipocyte development.

B. Pharm. and M. Pharm., University of Dhaka

Research focus

Research centers on the molecular mechanisms underlying adipogenesis, diabetes, metabolic syndrome, and inflammation. Recent work has increasingly focused on the therapeutic potential of natural compounds and phytochemicals, investigating their effects on oxidative stress and metabolic disorders using molecular and biochemical techniques.

Current research focus

The lab's ongoing work spans plant-based compound research, animal disease models, and molecular/biochemical analysis. Click a section to see the specific methods and tests involved.

Plant-based research + –

Extraction methods

  • Ultrasonication, maceration, hot extraction, and cold extraction

Phytochemical screening

  • Qualitative phytochemical screening
  • Quantitative screening — total phenolic content, total flavonoid content, anthocyanin content, tannin content, and proanthocyanidin content

Bioactivity testing

  • Antioxidant activity: DPPH, ABTS, FRAP, KMnO4, H2O2, methylene blue, TBARS, and nitric oxide scavenging assays
  • Antidiabetic activity: Alpha-amylase inhibition assay
Animal research + –

Disease models

  • High-fat diet, streptozotocin, fructose, or a combination of these — used to induce dyslipidemia, hyperlipidemia, lipid disorders, diabetes, or metabolic syndrome

Treatment models

  • Plant extracts or pure/isolated compounds
Tests performed with animal models + –

Before sacrifice

  • Oral Glucose Tolerance Test (OGTT)

After sacrifice — tissue, plasma, and fecal samples

  • Lipid profile: HDL, LDL, total cholesterol, triglycerides
  • Hepatic markers: AST, ALT, ALP
  • Oxidative stress markers: malondialdehyde, nitric oxide, superoxide dismutase, catalase, reduced glutathione, glutathione peroxidase, advanced oxidation protein products, myeloperoxidase
  • Insulin measurement
  • Free fatty acid measurement
  • Fecal fat analysis
  • Liver tissue lipid measurement (HDL, LDL, TG, TC)
  • Lipid extraction
  • RNA isolation, purification, and quantification
  • cDNA synthesis
  • Conventional and real-time PCR (qPCR)
  • Gel electrophoresis
Currently in development + –
  • Establishing in-house protocols for the beta-oxidation assay, CPT1 assay, and ATP/AMP ratio measurement
