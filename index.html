Dr. Ferdous Khan

Associate Professor, Department of Pharmaceutical Sciences, working at the intersection of natural product chemistry, formulation science, and antimicrobial research. This page exists so that colleagues, collaborators, and university admissions committees can quickly confirm a student's research background with this lab.

"Our work turns early-stage compound screening into research students can carry forward into their own careers — every project here is meant to produce something a student can stand behind, in a lab or a lecture hall, anywhere in the world."

01

Current research focus

The lab's ongoing work is organized around three areas. Every current or completed student project below maps to one or more of these.

Natural product screening

Isolation and bioactivity screening of plant-derived compounds against microbial and other biological targets.

Formulation science

Stability, delivery, and formulation testing for topical and oral pharmaceutical preparations.

Antimicrobial resistance

Evaluating novel and existing compounds for activity against resistant bacterial strains.
