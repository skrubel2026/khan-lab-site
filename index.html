Associate Professor · Pharmaceutical Sciences

Dr. Ferdous Khan

Associate Professor, Department of Pharmaceutical Sciences. Research centers on metabolic disease — dyslipidemia, diabetes, and non-alcoholic fatty liver disease (NAFLD) — investigated through plant-derived and antioxidant compounds, animal disease models, and molecular and biochemical analysis. This page exists so that colleagues, collaborators, and university admissions committees can quickly confirm a student's research background with this lab.

"We look at metabolic disease from both ends — screening what a compound does to the body, and using molecular tools to understand why it does it. Every student who passes through this lab leaves having worked across that whole pipeline."

01

Current research focus

The lab's ongoing work is organized around three areas. Every current or completed student project below maps to one or more of these.

Compound screening

Preparation of plant extracts, qualitative and quantitative phytochemical screening, and antioxidant and antidiabetic activity testing — evaluating whether a compound acts against dyslipidemia, diabetes, metabolic disorders, or NAFLD. Existing antioxidant compounds are also tested for molecular-level activity, alongside plant-derived extracts.

Disease modeling

Metabolic disease is induced using a high-fat diet, streptozotocin injection, or a combination of both. Fructose-induced fatty liver injury has also been used as a model for the disease.

Molecular & biochemical analysis

Students perform liver homogenization, RNA isolation, cDNA conversion, PCR, and qPCR under supervision, alongside insulin and free fatty acid measurement, lipid profiling, hepatic enzyme activity, in vivo antioxidant assays, and HDL, LDL, cholesterol, and triglyceride measurement in liver tissue.

Currently developing

The lab is currently working to establish protocols for measuring beta-oxidation, CPT1 assay, and ATP/AMP ratio.
